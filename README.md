# German Credit Risk Analysis & Predictive Modeling

Data-driven analysis of credit risk using customer financial profiles to support better lending decisions, risk management, and portfolio optimisation.

## Project Overview
This project analyses the German Credit dataset to understand borrower risk profiles and develop predictive insights for credit approval decisions.

The focus is not only on model performance, but on translating data into **risk-aware, business-relevant insights** that can support financial institutions in making more informed and scalable lending decisions.

This case study demonstrates how analytics can bridge the gap between **data science, risk governance, and business strategy**.

## Business Problem
Financial institutions face ongoing challenges in balancing:
- risk exposure  
- loan growth  
- customer acquisition  

Key issues include:
- inaccurate credit risk assessment leading to defaults  
- overly conservative approvals reducing revenue opportunities  
- lack of explainability in decision-making processes  

The objective is to:
**leverage data to improve credit decisioning, optimise risk-return trade-offs, and support sustainable portfolio growth**

## Objective
Develop analytical insights to:
- identify key drivers of credit risk  
- classify customers into risk categories  
- support credit approval decisions  
- improve risk segmentation strategies  
- enable more consistent and data-driven lending frameworks  

## Dataset
- German Credit dataset  
- Customer demographic, financial, and behavioural attributes  

Key variables include:
- credit amount  
- loan duration  
- employment status  
- savings and account status  
- repayment history  

## Approach
The project follows a structured analytics workflow:

1. Business understanding of credit risk dynamics  
2. Exploratory data analysis (EDA)  
3. Feature engineering and preprocessing  
4. Model development for risk classification  
5. Evaluation using appropriate performance metrics  
6. Interpretation of results for business decision-making  

The focus is on **interpretability, robustness, and alignment with real-world financial risk practices**.

## Key Results
- Identified key variables influencing creditworthiness  
- Developed classification models to distinguish between low-risk and high-risk customers  
- Evaluated model performance using appropriate metrics (e.g., recall, accuracy)  
- Highlighted trade-offs between false positives and false negatives in lending decisions  
- Provided insights into improving risk segmentation and approval strategies
